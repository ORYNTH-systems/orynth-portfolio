## Layer I — Constitutional Theory

The theoretical foundation establishes the mathematical and constitutional principles governing admissible execution, integrity preservation, continuity, and bounded computation.

Representative works include:

- Integrity Preservation Theory (IPT)
- Constitutional State Governance
- Companion mathematical specifications

---

## Layer II — Constitutional Architectures

These specifications translate constitutional principles into executable architectural models.

Examples include:

- Unified Agency Architecture (UAA)
- Adaptive Orchestration Management System (AOMS)
- Constraint-Integrated Glyphic Control System (CIGCS)
- Proof of Block (POB)
- Execution Identity Preservation (EIP)

---

## Layer III — Reference Laboratories

Reference laboratories provide deterministic implementations, benchmark corpora, and executable demonstrations that validate the published specifications.

---

## Layer IV — Runtime Systems

Runtime engines enforce constitutional evaluation during execution rather than relying solely on pre-execution authorization.

---

## Layer V — Domain Applications

Domain-specific deployments apply constitutional computing to industries including healthcare, financial infrastructure, robotics, simulation, digital twins, and future autonomous systems.

---

## Guiding Principle

Every layer exists to provide stronger evidence than the layer above it:

Theory ? Specification ? Reference Implementation ? Runtime ? Deployment
