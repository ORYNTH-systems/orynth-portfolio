# ORYNTH Research Timeline

## Overview

This timeline documents the evolution of the ORYNTH research program. Each milestone builds upon earlier constitutional foundations while introducing new architectural capabilities.

---

# Phase I — Foundational Theory

## Integrity Preservation Theory (IPT)

Focus:

- Integrity as a measurable computational property.
- Continuity under bounded change.
- Formal preservation and recoverability.

---

## Constitutional State Governance

Focus:

- Admissibility as a primitive of computation.
- Constitutional evaluation of execution.
- Separation of authorization and execution eligibility.

---

# Phase II — Constitutional Architectures

## Unified Agency Architecture (UAA)

Established execution admissibility as a runtime constitutional decision.

---

## Execution Identity Preservation (EIP)

Formalized execution continuity and identity preservation across state transitions.

---

## Adaptive Orchestration Management System (AOMS)

Introduced orchestration-governed execution eligibility for autonomous and distributed systems.

---

## Constraint-Integrated Glyphic Control System (CIGCS)

Applied constitutional governance to render authorization artifacts, admissibility, and enforcement through executable benchmark corpora.

---

## Proof of Block (POB)

Extends constitutional computing with deterministic proof that prohibited executions did not occur.

Status: In Development.
